Canon in D
Johann Pachelbel · c. 1680

Pachelbel's Canon is one of the most recognizable pieces in classical music. Written around 1680, it features a repeating bass line over which three violins play interlocking melodies — a technique called a ground bass.

It was largely forgotten after Pachelbel's time and only became widely known in the 1970s when a famous recording brought it to new audiences worldwide.
